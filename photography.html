Photography

How it started for me

My interest in photography started in early high school when my grandpa let me borrow his old Canon 5D mkII. Which is a big DSLR camera that was way nicer than any camera I had ever used before. I started taking pictures with my friend Ian, who also wanted to learn about cameras. We would watch endless YouTube tutorials about how cameras work, how an exposure is made, and how to manually choose the setting to achieve fun results. We would go on hikes and camping trips to practice the techniques we learned about. Like many beginning photographers, we became especially interested in long exposure and low light photography. It felt amazing to be able to capture the beauty of the night sky with even more detail and vibrance than you could see with your own eyes.

The technical side of photography

Most of my hobbies are technical in nature, and photography is no exception. I love learning about the physics of light and how it interacts with the camera. Balancing the exposure triangle of ISO, aperture, and shutter speed is a fun challenge that can be applied to many different situations. Combining the art of composition with the science of exposure is what makes photography so interesting to me. Learning to edit my photos was a whole other challenge that helped me learn about using computers and managing files, which was beneficial as I began learning about computer science. You'll see in the FPV Drones page of this website how my interest in technical hobbies has led me into building and flying First-Person-View drones.
